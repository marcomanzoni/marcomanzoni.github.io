Marco Manzoni (born in 1994) is Assistant Professor at Politecnico di Milano, where he conducts research and teaching in radar remote sensing and advanced signal processing. He received his M.Sc. degree (cum laude) in Telecommunications Engineering in 2018 and his Ph.D. (cum laude) in Information Technology in 2022, both from Politecnico di Milano.

His research focuses on Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) systems and algorithms across spaceborne, airborne, UAV-based, and automotive platforms. His work includes automotive MIMO-SAR imaging, motion estimation and compensation, autofocusing techniques, SAR interferometry (InSAR), tomography, and radiometric calibration, with applications ranging from Earth observation to intelligent transportation and structural monitoring. He has contributed to international projects such as the ESA Sentinel-1 and BIOMASS commissioning phases. He previously worked on Joint Communication and Sensing (ISAC/JC&S) topics and served as Chair of the IEEE Automotive SAR Standard Working Group; these activities have now concluded, and his current research is fully focused on radar sensing.

His research has been recognized with the Best Paper Award at EuSAR 2024, the Best Paper Award at the 2022 Mediterranean Microwave Symposium, the 2023 PIERS Young Scientist Award, and the 2022 Dimitris N. Chorafas Award.

Alongside research, he is actively involved in teaching. He is currently Head of the Ph.D. courses *The Copernicus Green Revolution for Sustainable Development* and *Radar Remote Sensing: Principles, Applications, and Integration with Communication Systems*. He serves as teaching assistant for *Remote Sensing for Earth Observation and Surveillance* (M.Sc. in Aerospace Engineering), *Segnali per le Comunicazioni* (B.Sc. in Computer Science), *Radar Imaging* (M.Sc. in Telecommunications Engineering), and *Earth Observation* (M.Sc. in Geoinformatics). In previous years, he was teaching assistant for *Fondamenti di Segnali e Trasmissione* (B.Sc. in Engineering Electronics).
